#### **Session 2: Consultative Selling Framework**
**Timing:** Wednesday 5/3, 09:00-11:00 (All regions)
**Trainer:** Sales Director
**Duration:** 2 hours
**Format:** Workshop + Role-play

**Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Consultative Selling Model (30 min)**
- Traditional selling vs. Consultative selling:
  - Traditional: "Here's the product, buy it"
  - Consultative: "Tell me about your customers, let me propose solutions"
- 4-step framework:
  1. **Diagnosis** (Ask questions)
     - "What's your best-selling category?"
     - "What margin targets do you have?"
     - "Who's your target demographic?"
  2. **Discovery** (Listen actively)
     - What challenges are they facing?
     - What are their pain points?
     - What's their seasonal demand?
  3. **Prescription** (Recommend solution)
     - Product bundles tailored to their need
     - Pricing/incentive structure
     - Delivery/support terms
  4. **Commitment** (Close the deal)
     - Clear next steps
     - Order confirmation
     - Follow-up schedule

**Module 2: Account Analysis Framework (30 min)**
- Customer segmentation:
  - **Luxury-focused accounts:** Position Bolon (premium models)
  - **Volume-driven accounts:** Molsion + Qina mix
  - **Kids/family accounts:** Qina kids line
  - **Budget-conscious accounts:** Qina entry-level + Serova (coming April)
- Pricing psychology:
  - Never lead with price → Lead with value
  - Bundle discounts feel better than individual discounts
  - Volume targets = credibility with KA
- Objection handling:
  - *"Your products are expensive"* → "Compare to competitors on quality & margin"
  - *"No inventory space"* → "Loan demo, build traffic first"
  - *"Can't sell that much"* → "Start with Bundle A, grow together"

**Module 3: Real Role-Play Scenarios (45 min)**
- **Scenario 1:** Tier A account (high-volume, margin-conscious)
  - Rep: Pitch Bolon + Molsion bundle at 4% discount
  - KA concern: *"Need more margin to justify shelf space"*
  - Rep response: Explain margin structure + sell-through pace
- **Scenario 2:** Tier B account (growth-phase)
  - Rep: Introduce Qina refreshed line
  - KA concern: *"Never heard of Qina, why should I stock it?"*
  - Rep response: Market data, target demographic, entry price
- **Scenario 3:** New account prospect
  - Rep: Consultative discovery call
  - KA concern: *"Why switch from my current supplier?"*
  - Rep response: Product quality, support, incentive structure

   - Healthy: Stable or growing (+5% YoY)
   - Warning: Declining (-5% to -15%)
   - Red flag: Declining >15%

3. **Product mix**
   - Healthy: Diverse (30% Bolon + 40% Molsion + 20% Qina + 10% other)

   - Healthy: 40%+ (inventory turning)
   - Warning: 25-40% (slower movement)
   - Red flag: <25% (stagnant inventory)

5. **Competitive pressure**
   - Healthy: No competitive products mentioned
   - Warning: Customer mentioning competitors' pricing/products
   - Red flag: Lost shelf space to competitor

**B. Monitoring Schedule:**
- Monthly review: Pull data on 5 health indicators
- Quarterly business review: Deep-dive discussion with account
- Document in CRM: Trends over time

**Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Advanced Objection Handling (40 min)**

**Common objections & rebuttals:**

| Objection | Root cause | Rebuttal | Follow-up |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| *"Your prices are too high"* | Competitor comparison | *"Compare quality & margin. We've proven 56% ST with Molsion."* | Margin discussion |
| *"No shelf space"* | Category overstock | *"Let's consolidate SKU count, free up 20% space, and optimize profitability."* | Assortment review |

**Module 2: Negotiation Framework (40 min)**

**5-step negotiation model:**
1. **Preparation** (before call)
   - Know account's situation (current spend, products, pain points)
   - Set negotiation anchors (bottom-line price, terms, volume)
   - Plan opening offer (position it as WIN-WIN)

2. **Opening** (set the tone)
   - Collaborative: *"Let's find a solution that works for both of us"*
   - NOT adversarial: *"Here's my final offer, take it or leave it"*
   - Build rapport: Acknowledge their business challenges

3. **Exploration** (understand their needs)
   - Ask questions: *"What's most important to you in a supplier?"*
   - Listen for priorities (price vs. service vs. uniqueness)
   - Find the "trade-off" (what can we give that costs us little but is valuable to them?)

4. **Bargaining** (exchange value)
   - Trade-offs: *"If we offer 2% discount, can you commit to 50-unit monthly minimum?"*
   - Package deals: *"Bundle pricing: Bolon + Molsion together = better discount"*
   - NOT simple discounting: "Always give something to get something"

5. **Closing** (confirm & document)
   - Summarize: *"So we have 30 units Bolon, 40 Molsion, 2% discount, net 45 terms—correct?"*
   - Written confirmation: Email recap within 24 hours
   - Next steps: *"Delivery April 15. Check-in call April 20. Sound good?"*

**Module 3: Real Case Studies (40 min)**

**Case 1: Premium account (Việt Tín HN)**
- Situation: Wants to increase Bolon order from 20 to 30 units, but wants 3% discount
- Reps' dilemma: Can't afford 3%, but don't want to lose this top account
- Solution options:
  - Option A: Offer 1.5% discount + free display + exclusive "Bolon Premier" shelf support
  - Option B: Counter with 2% discount IF they commit to 60-unit quarterly contract (volume guarantee)
  - Option C: No discount, but offer 90-day payment terms (reduce their cash flow burden)
- Role-play: Rep negotiates with trainer (playing the account)

**Case 2: New account prospect (Serova launch)**
- Situation: Small shop (first-time buy), wants to pilot Serova with low-risk terms
- Reps' dilemma: Small order (10 units) has lower margin, but it's a new account
- Solution: Offer Serova pilot terms = low MOQ, 30-day free consignment, weekly support calls
- Role-play: Rep pitches Serova pilot, handles objections

**Case 3: Housebrand conversion (Miền Nam)**
- Situation: Account has 30 Housebrand units, only 40% sell-through, wants to clear
- Reps' dilemma: Account frustrated with Housebrand, open to Serova, but wants to negotiate price on both
- Solution: Trade allowance approach = Take back unsold Housebrand, credit 80% toward Serova order, net result neutral for customer but improves our inventory
- Role-play: Rep manages trade-in negotiation

**Module 4: Pricing Psychology (20 min)**
- Don't anchor on price (anchor on value)
- Bundle discounts > Individual discounts (psychological preference)
- Absolute discount vs. % discount:
  - *"1% off" = feels small*
  - *"3,500 VND off" = feels bigger even if same $*
- Volume thresholds (create "ladder"):
  - 10-20 units: 1% discount
  - 20-30 units: 2% discount
  - 30%+ units: 3% discount
  - Encourages step-up buying

**Role-play practice (30 min)**
- 3-4 reps volunteer to negotiate real scenarios
